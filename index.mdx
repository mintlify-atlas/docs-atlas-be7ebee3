## What is MediaWiki?

MediaWiki is a free and open-source wiki framework written in PHP. It powers Wikipedia and thousands of other wikis across the web. As a framework, it provides:

- A **hook system** that allows extensions to modify nearly every aspect of the application
- A **service container** (`MediaWikiServices`) for dependency injection across all subsystems
- A **REST API** and **Action API** for programmatic access to wiki content
- A **ResourceLoader** for optimized JavaScript and CSS delivery
- A **parser** that converts wikitext markup to HTML, with support for custom content models
- A **skin system** for fully customizing the rendered HTML output

<Info>
  MediaWiki requires PHP 8.2 or later and supports MySQL/MariaDB, PostgreSQL, and SQLite databases.
</Info>

## Getting started paths

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Extension Developer">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Install MediaWiki">
        Follow the [installation guide](/installation/setup) to set up a local development environment.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Understand the architecture">
        Read the [architecture overview](/architecture) to understand request flow, the service container, and the hook system.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create an extension">
        Follow the [extension overview](/extensions/overview) to create your first extension with `extension.json`.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Register hooks">
        Learn how to [register and implement hooks](/extensions/hooks) to respond to MediaWiki events.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add services">
        Use [dependency injection](/extensions/services) to make your extension testable and maintainable.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API Integrator">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Choose your API">
        MediaWiki provides two APIs. The [REST API](/api/rest/overview) is modern and RESTful. The [Action API](/api/action/overview) is more feature-complete and widely used.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Read pages">
        Use [GET /v1/page/{title}](/api/rest/pages) or [action=parse](/api/action/query) to retrieve page content.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Authenticate for writes">
        Follow the [authentication guide](/api/action/authentication) to obtain tokens for editing and other write operations.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Edit content">
        Use [action=edit](/api/action/edit) to create and modify pages programmatically.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Skin Developer">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Understand skins">
        Read the [skins overview](/skins/overview) to understand how skins render the page output.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set up your skin">
        Follow the [skin development guide](/skins/development) to create a `skin.json` and a base skin class.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Build templates">
        Use [Mustache templates](/skins/templating) to define the HTML structure of your skin.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
